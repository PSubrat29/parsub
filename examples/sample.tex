\section{Introduction}
We aim to analyze the trajectory of a projectile launched at an angle with initial velocity.

\section{Equations of Motion}
The parametric equations for projectile motion are:
\begin{align}
x(t) &= v_0 \cos(\theta) t \\
y(t) &= v_0 \sin(\theta) t - \frac{1}{2} g t^2
\end{align}
where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $x(t)$ is the horizontal position at time $t$
\item $y(t)$ is the vertical position at time $t$
\item $v_0$ is the initial launch velocity
\item $\theta$ is the launch angle (radians)
\item $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity ($9.81  m/s^2$)
\item $t$ is time
\end{itemize}

\section{Objective}
We want to:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Plot the trajectory for different launch angles
\item Find the maximum range as a function of launch angle
\item Compute the time of flight
\end{enumerate}

\section{Trajectory Equation}
Eliminating $t$, we get the trajectory equation:
\begin{equation}
y = x \tan(\theta) - \frac{g x^2}{2 v_0^2 \cos^2(\theta)}
\end{equation}

\section{Range Equation}
The range $R$ (horizontal distance when $y=0$) is given by:
\begin{equation}
R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}
\end{equation}

\section{Time of Flight}
The time of flight $T$ is:
\begin{equation}
T = \frac{2 v_0 \sin(\theta)}{g}
\end{equation}

\section{Parameters}
In our analysis, we will vary:
\begin{itemize}
\item Launch angle $\theta$ from $0$ to $\frac{\pi}{2}$ radians
\item Initial velocity $v_0$ = 20 m/s (fixed)
\item Gravity $g$ = 9.81 m/s^2 (fixed)
\end{itemize}
